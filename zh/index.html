Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about ICBC treatment

Can I book ICBC treatment with only a claim number? +
To book ICBC-funded treatment, you generally need your ICBC claim number and Personal Health Number (PHN). A clinic may request additional details to confirm your booking.
What treatments are covered by ICBC? +
For eligible claims, ICBC pre-approved treatment may include chiropractic, physiotherapy, registered massage therapy, acupuncture, kinesiology and clinical counselling. Additional treatment may be available if ICBC approves it.
How long can I receive ICBC treatment after a car accident? +
For eligible claims, ICBC pre-approves specified treatments during the first 12 weeks after a crash. If you need further care, speak with your health care provider and ICBC recovery specialist about next steps.
What happens if my recovery takes longer than 12 weeks? +
If you need treatment beyond the first 12 weeks, your health care provider can work with your ICBC recovery specialist to determine whether an extension is available for your situation.
How is ICBC treatment different for children after a car accident? +
Treatment is adapted to a child's age and development, with parents involved throughout recovery. Initial Family Therapy is included to support both the child and family.
How is car accident treatment adapted during pregnancy? +
Treatment techniques, positioning, and exercises are adapted to your stage of pregnancy and symptoms to help keep care safe and comfortable.
How is car accident treatment adapted for seniors? +
Treatment is adapted to your mobility, overall health, and recovery pace, with a focus on restoring daily function safely.
What languages do your partner clinics support? +
Our partner clinics provide care in English, Mandarin, and Cantonese.
