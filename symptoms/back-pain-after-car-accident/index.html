Why get assessed early?

Back pain sometimes becomes worse over the first few days after a collision. An early assessment can help identify the cause of your pain and guide the most appropriate treatment before symptoms become more persistent.

People Also Ask

Back Pain FAQ

Why does my back hurt if I felt fine right after the accident? +
Back pain can show up hours or days later because adrenaline, inflammation, and muscle guarding can mask symptoms at first. Even a collision that feels minor can strain the muscles, joints, discs, ligaments, or nerves in the back.
Is my back pain muscle strain, joint irritation, or nerve-related? +
It can be hard to tell without an assessment. Back pain after a crash may involve muscle strain, joint irritation, disc-related symptoms, or nerve irritation. A chiropractic assessment can help identify how the spine and surrounding joints may be irritated and guide the right recovery plan.
When should I get checked urgently? +
Seek urgent medical care if back pain comes with new or worsening leg weakness, numbness in the groin or saddle area, trouble walking, fever, major trauma, or loss of bladder or bowel control. If symptoms are milder but not improving, book an assessment promptly.
Should I wait and see, or start chiropractic care? +
If pain lasts more than a few days, limits bending or walking, affects sleep or work, or comes with hip, buttock, or leg symptoms, it is worth getting assessed. Early chiropractic care can help improve spinal movement, reduce joint stiffness, and prevent symptoms from becoming more persistent.
What happens during the first chiropractic assessment? +
Your chiropractor will ask about the crash, your symptoms, movement limits, leg symptoms, work demands, and daily activities. They may check back and hip range of motion, posture, joint mobility, muscle sensitivity, and nerve-related signs before recommending a treatment plan.
Can back pain cause hip, buttock, or leg symptoms? +
Yes. Back strain or irritated joints can refer pain into the hips, buttocks, or legs. Numbness, tingling, shooting pain, or weakness may suggest nerve irritation and should be assessed.
Does ICBC cover treatment for back pain after a car accident? +
In many cases, ICBC may cover treatment for back pain, lower back strain, hip pain, leg symptoms, or related soft tissue injuries after a motor vehicle accident. Coverage depends on your claim, symptoms, and assessment. Participating clinics may also offer direct billing.
How long does back pain take to recover? +
Recovery time varies. Some people improve within days or weeks, while others need a longer plan if pain, stiffness, leg symptoms, or movement limits persist. Your recovery depends on the severity of the injury, previous back issues, work demands, sleep, stress, and how early treatment begins.
