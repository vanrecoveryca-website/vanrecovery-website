When to seek care

Should you seek care?

You should consider an assessment if your headaches started after the accident and any of the following apply.

Headaches began after the collision
You feel dizzy, foggy, or sensitive to light
Headaches are getting worse instead of better
Symptoms affect work, sleep, screens, or driving

Not sure if it is serious?

If headaches are new, worsening, or affecting daily life, it is reasonable to get assessed. Early care can help identify whether symptoms may be related to whiplash, concussion, neck tension, stress, or other accident-related factors.

People Also Ask

Common questions about headaches after a car accident

Why do headaches appear the day after a car accident? +
Headaches can appear 24–72 hours after a collision as inflammation, muscle guarding, neck strain, stress, or concussion-related symptoms become more noticeable.
Are headaches after a car accident caused by whiplash? +
Whiplash can contribute to headaches after a motor vehicle accident, especially when the neck muscles and joints are irritated. Headaches may also be related to concussion, stress, sleep disruption, or other causes.
When should I see a healthcare provider? +
You should consider an assessment if headaches are getting worse, happen with dizziness or nausea, affect concentration, limit screen use, or interfere with work, driving, sleep, or daily activities.
Can ICBC cover treatment for headaches? +
In many cases, yes. Eligible ICBC claims may include treatment such as physiotherapy, massage therapy, active rehabilitation, and other approved recovery services.
How long do post-accident headaches usually last? +
Recovery varies depending on the cause and severity of symptoms. Some headaches improve within a few weeks, while others may require ongoing rehabilitation, symptom monitoring, and follow-up care.
Can I start treatment before my adjuster contacts me? +
In many cases, yes. After reporting your accident and receiving an ICBC claim number, you may be able to begin treatment without waiting for your adjuster to contact you.
