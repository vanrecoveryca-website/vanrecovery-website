What You May Feel

Common symptoms after a crash

Neck pain after a car accident may feel tight, sharp, heavy, stiff, or difficult to move.

  • Neck stiffness or tightness
  • Pain when turning your head
  • Headaches after the accident
  • Shoulder or upper back tension
  • Muscle spasms or tenderness
  • Difficulty sleeping comfortably

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions about neck pain after a car accident

Why does my neck hurt more the next day? +
Neck pain can become more noticeable 24–72 hours after a collision as muscle guarding, inflammation, joint irritation, or whiplash-related soft tissue injury develops.
Can whiplash heal by itself? +
Mild symptoms may improve with time, but persistent pain, headaches, dizziness, numbness, weakness, or reduced movement should be assessed by a qualified healthcare provider.
How long should I wait before seeing a physiotherapist? +
If symptoms are not improving, affecting sleep, limiting movement, or worsening after the accident, an early assessment can help guide safe recovery.
Should I use heat or ice for neck pain? +
It depends on your symptoms and injury stage. Some people prefer ice for early soreness, while others respond better to gentle heat for muscle tightness. Avoid anything that worsens pain, dizziness, or nerve symptoms.
Can ICBC cover massage therapy for neck pain? +
ICBC may cover eligible massage therapy for accident-related neck pain depending on claim eligibility, treatment period, and clinical need.
