Symptoms & Causes

Common neck pain symptoms after a car accident

Neck pain after a crash may feel sharp, tight, stiff, heavy, or difficult to move. Symptoms can appear right away or develop over the next several hours or days.

✓ Neck stiffness or tightness
✓ Pain when turning the head
✓ Headaches after the accident
✓ Shoulder or upper back tension
✓ Muscle spasms or tenderness
✓ Difficulty sleeping comfortably

Why It Happens

Why neck pain can happen after a collision

A sudden impact can force the head and neck to move quickly. This may irritate muscles, joints, ligaments, nerves, or other soft tissues around the cervical spine.

Whiplash

Rapid neck movement after impact.

Muscle Strain

Soft tissue tension or guarding.

Joint Irritation

Cervical spine stress or stiffness.

Nerve Signs

Numbness, tingling, or weakness.

ICBC Coverage

Can ICBC cover treatment for neck pain?

ICBC may cover eligible treatment for accident-related neck pain and whiplash symptoms. During the first 12 weeks after a crash, many patients can access eligible treatment without a physician referral or prior ICBC approval, depending on claim eligibility and treatment type.

12 Weeks

Initial recovery window may include pre-approved treatment access.

No Referral

Many patients can start eligible care without a doctor referral.

Direct Billing

Eligible treatment may be billed directly to ICBC.

People Also Ask

Common questions about neck pain after a car accident

Yes. Neck pain, stiffness, headaches, and shoulder tension may appear immediately or develop several hours or days after a collision.

Seek urgent medical care if symptoms are severe or concerning. For many non-emergency ICBC injuries, patients may begin eligible treatment with a physiotherapist during the first 12 weeks.

Gentle movement may help some patients, but exercise should match the injury stage and symptoms. Avoid activities that worsen pain, dizziness, numbness, or headaches.

Imaging is not always needed for mild neck pain. A doctor may recommend imaging if there are red flags such as severe pain, neurological symptoms, trauma concerns, or worsening symptoms.

ICBC may cover eligible massage therapy for accident-related neck pain depending on claim eligibility, treatment period, and clinical need.
