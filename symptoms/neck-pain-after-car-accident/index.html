Why get assessed early?

Neck pain sometimes becomes worse over the first few days after a collision. An early assessment can help identify the cause of your pain and guide the most appropriate treatment before symptoms become more persistent.

People Also Ask

Neck Pain FAQ

Why does my neck hurt if I felt fine right after the accident? +
Neck pain can show up hours or days later because adrenaline, inflammation, and muscle guarding can mask symptoms at first. Even a collision that feels minor can strain the muscles, joints, ligaments, or nerves in the neck.
Is my neck pain whiplash or just muscle strain? +
It can be hard to tell without an assessment. Whiplash commonly involves neck pain, stiffness, reduced movement, headaches, shoulder or upper back pain, and sometimes numbness or tingling. A physiotherapy assessment can help identify what structures may be irritated and guide the right recovery plan.
When should I get checked urgently? +
Seek urgent medical care if neck pain comes with severe or worsening headache, dizziness, fainting, confusion, vision changes, weakness, numbness, tingling down the arm, trouble walking, or loss of bladder or bowel control. If symptoms are milder but not improving, book an assessment promptly.
Should I wait and see, or start physiotherapy? +
If pain lasts more than a few days, limits neck movement, affects sleep or work, or comes with headaches or shoulder pain, it is worth getting assessed. Early physiotherapy can help restore movement, reduce muscle guarding, and prevent symptoms from becoming more persistent.
What happens during the first physiotherapy assessment? +
Your physiotherapist will ask about the crash, your symptoms, movement limits, headaches, work demands, and daily activities. They may check neck range of motion, strength, posture, joint and muscle sensitivity, and nerve-related signs before recommending a treatment plan.
Can neck pain cause headaches, shoulder pain, or arm symptoms? +
Yes. Neck strain can refer pain into the head, shoulders, upper back, or arms. Headaches can come from irritated neck joints or muscles. Numbness, tingling, or weakness may suggest nerve irritation and should be assessed.
Does ICBC cover treatment for neck pain after a car accident? +
In many cases, ICBC may cover treatment for neck pain, whiplash, headaches, or related soft tissue injuries after a motor vehicle accident. Coverage depends on your claim, symptoms, and assessment. Participating clinics may also offer direct billing.
How long does neck pain take to recover? +
Recovery time varies. Some people improve within days or weeks, while others need a longer plan if pain, stiffness, headaches, or movement limits persist. Your recovery depends on the severity of the injury, previous neck issues, work demands, sleep, stress, and how early treatment begins.
