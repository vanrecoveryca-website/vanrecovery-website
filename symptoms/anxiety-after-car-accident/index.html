People Also Ask

Anxiety & Stress FAQ

I wasn't seriously injured. Why do I still feel anxious? +
A collision can affect emotional well-being even when physical injuries are minor. Anxiety, fear, or sleep problems may appear days or weeks later as your mind processes the experience. If these feelings continue or begin affecting daily life, an assessment can help determine the right support.
Why did my anxiety start days after the accident? +
Anxiety or stress does not always appear immediately after a collision. Many people focus on physical recovery first and notice sleep difficulties, driving fear, or stress later. This delayed response is a common reaction after a traumatic event.
Is it normal to be afraid of driving after a car accident? +
Yes. Feeling nervous about driving—or even riding in a car—is common after a collision. If the fear doesn't improve or begins affecting your daily routine, counselling may help you regain confidence.
How do I know if it's anxiety or PTSD? +
Both anxiety and PTSD can develop after a traumatic event and may share similar symptoms. PTSD often involves persistent nightmares, flashbacks, avoidance, or feeling constantly on edge. A clinical assessment can help identify what's causing your symptoms and recommend appropriate support.
Will these feelings go away on their own? +
Some people recover naturally over time, while others continue to experience anxiety or fear long after the accident. Early assessment can help identify your recovery needs and recommend appropriate support if needed.
Should I see a counsellor even if my physical injuries are improving? +
Yes. Physical and emotional recovery do not always happen at the same pace. In our clinical experience, people often recover more completely when both physical and emotional symptoms are addressed together.
Does ICBC cover counselling after a car accident? +
In many cases, ICBC may cover counselling for anxiety, stress, sleep problems, or driving fear related to a motor vehicle accident. Treatment recommendations depend on your symptoms, claim, and clinical assessment. Participating clinics may also offer direct billing.
My child seems different after the accident. Is that normal? +
Yes. Children may respond differently after a collision and may become fearful, withdrawn, have nightmares, or avoid riding in a car. Family counselling can help parents understand these changes and support their child's recovery.

Related Symptoms

Other symptoms after a car accident

Anxiety and stress can appear together with other post-accident symptoms.

Headaches After a Car Accident

Headaches may appear after neck strain, whiplash, concussion, or muscle tension.

Back Pain After a Car Accident

Back discomfort may occur when the spine absorbs collision-related force.

Neck Pain After a Car Accident

Neck pain, including shoulder pain, may result from whiplash or soft tissue injury.
