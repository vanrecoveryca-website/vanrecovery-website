    Pre-approved ICBC treatments available after a car accident

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Common questions about ICBC treatment, physiotherapy, counselling, direct billing, and car accident recovery in Vancouver and Richmond.

    Most eligible ICBC patients do not need to pay upfront for approved treatment sessions. Physiotherapy, massage therapy, counselling, kinesiology, and rehabilitation services may be billed directly to ICBC depending on your claim status and treatment eligibility.
    Under ICBC Enhanced Care, many patients are pre-approved for treatment during the first 12 weeks after a crash, regardless of who caused the accident. This recovery period may include physiotherapy, massage therapy, kinesiology, counselling, psychology, and other approved recovery services.
    If your injuries require a longer recovery period, you may qualify for an ICBC benefit extension. Your health care provider can recommend additional treatment and communicate those needs to your ICBC recovery specialist. For more complex injuries, ICBC may request a Comprehensive Medical Assessment (CMA).
    Yes. ICBC commonly covers physiotherapy for injuries related to motor vehicle accidents, including whiplash, neck pain, headaches, stiffness, back pain, dizziness, and mobility limitations.
    Yes. ICBC may cover counselling, psychology, and emotional recovery support for symptoms such as anxiety, trauma, stress, sleep issues, fear of driving, and emotional distress after a car accident.
    Common symptoms after a motor vehicle accident include neck pain, back pain, headaches, dizziness, stiffness, muscle soreness, anxiety, sleep issues, reduced mobility, and emotional stress. Some symptoms may appear hours or days after the crash.
    Patients looking for both physical rehabilitation and emotional recovery support can access integrated ICBC-related care through Aetheris Integrated Health in Vancouver West Side and Pegasus Integrated Health in Richmond.
