    Frequently Asked Questions

    Common questions about ICBC treatment

    Can I book ICBC treatment with only a claim number? +
    Yes, in many cases. You can usually book your first appointment with your ICBC claim number. Clinics may also ask for a valid photo ID to verify your identity and basic accident details.
    Can I start treatment before my adjuster contacts me? +
    In many cases, yes. After reporting your accident to ICBC and receiving your claim number, you can often begin treatment without waiting for an adjuster to contact you. If you're unsure about your eligibility, the clinic can help guide you through the next steps.
    Do I need a doctor's referral for ICBC treatment? +
    In many cases, no. Eligible patients can usually begin treatment after receiving an ICBC claim number. If a referral or additional documentation is needed, the clinic can let you know before your appointment.
    Do I need to pay upfront for ICBC treatment? +
    In many cases, no. Eligible ICBC treatment can often be billed directly to ICBC, so most patients do not need to pay upfront. Coverage depends on your claim and treatment eligibility.
    What treatments are covered by ICBC? +
    Depending on your claim and treatment eligibility, ICBC coverage may include chiropractic, physiotherapy, registered massage therapy, acupuncture, kinesiology, and clinical counselling. Additional treatment may be approved if it is medically necessary.
    How quickly can I start treatment after a car accident? +
    Most patients can begin treatment within 2–4 days after contacting one of our partner clinics. The earliest available appointment depends on the treatment type, clinic location, and practitioner availability.
    How long can I receive ICBC treatment after a car accident? +
    Under ICBC Enhanced Care, eligible patients are generally pre-approved for treatment during the first 12 weeks after a car accident. If additional treatment is medically necessary, your healthcare provider may recommend ongoing care based on your recovery needs.
    What happens if my recovery takes longer than 12 weeks? +
    If you still need treatment after the initial 12-week recovery period, your healthcare provider may submit a treatment plan to ICBC for additional care. Approval is based on your recovery progress and clinical needs.
