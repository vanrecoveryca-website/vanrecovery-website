    Common questions about ICBC treatment

    In many cases, yes. After reporting your accident to ICBC and receiving a claim number, you may be able to begin the treatment process without waiting for an adjuster to contact you.
    Please bring your ICBC claim number and one piece of government-issued photo ID. Information about your accident date and symptoms may also be helpful.
    In many cases, eligible ICBC treatment can be billed directly to ICBC. Most patients do not need to pay upfront, although coverage and eligibility may vary depending on the treatment provided.
    Appointment availability depends on the clinic and service requested. Acupuncture and chiropractic appointments are often available within 2–3 days, while massage therapy appointments may take approximately 3–4 days depending on practitioner availability.
    Under ICBC Enhanced Care, many patients are pre-approved for treatment during the first 12 weeks after a crash, regardless of who caused the accident. This recovery period may include physiotherapy, massage therapy, kinesiology, counselling, psychology, and other approved recovery services.
    After the initial 12-week pre-approved treatment period, your healthcare provider may submit a treatment request to ICBC if additional care is clinically necessary. Approval decisions are made on a case-by-case basis based on your recovery progress and treatment needs.
